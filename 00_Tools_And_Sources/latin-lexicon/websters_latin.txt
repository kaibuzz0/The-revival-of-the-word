Classical and Ecclesiastical Latin Dictionary

About Latin:
  - Language of ancient Rome and the Roman Empire
  - Classical Latin: 100 BCE - 200 CE
  - Ecclesiastical Latin: Church and Vulgate Bible (400 CE-present)
  - Extensive case system (nominative, genitive, dative, accusative, ablative, vocative)
  - Five declensions, four conjugations

Pronunciation Guide:
  ā = long a (father)    ă = short a (cat)
  ē = long e (say)       ĕ = short e (pet)
  ī = long i (machine)   ĭ = short i (pin)
  ō = long o (tone)      ŏ = short o (hot)
  ū = long u (rule)      ŭ = short u (put)
  c = always hard k (cat)   g = always hard g (go)
  v = w or v               j = y (as in yes)
