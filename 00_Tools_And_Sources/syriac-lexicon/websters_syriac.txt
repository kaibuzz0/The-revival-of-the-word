Syriac-English Dictionary (Peshitta)

About Syriac:
  - Dialect of Aramaic (Eastern Aramaic)
  - Language of the Peshitta (Syriac Bible)
  - Classical period: 200-700 CE
  - Three scripts: Estrangela, Serto (West), East Syriac (Nestorian)
  - Written right to left like Hebrew and Arabic

Pronunciation Guide:
  ܐ = ʾ (glottal stop or silent)
  ܒ = b (soft) or ḇ (hard/v, when marked with hard dot)
  ܓ = g (soft) or ḡ (hard/gh, when marked)
  ܕ = d (soft) or ḏ (hard/dh, when marked)
  ܚ = ḥ (voiceless pharyngeal, like Arabic ح)
  ܛ = ṭ (emphatic t)
  ܥ = ʿ (voiced pharyngeal, like Arabic ع)
  ܨ = ṣ (emphatic s)
  ܩ = q (emphatic k)
  ܪ = r (rolled)
  ܫ = š (sh)
  ܬ = t (soft) or ṯ (hard/th, when marked)
