About Old Georgian:
  - Kartvelian (South Caucasian) language family
  - UNRELATED to Indo-European or Semitic languages
  - Language of the ancient Georgian Orthodox Church
  - Three scripts: Asomtavruli (ancient), Nuskhuri (medieval), Mkhedruli (modern)
  - Unique alphabet with 33 letters
  - Agglutinative language (like Turkish, Finnish)

Pronunciation Guide:
  ა = a (father)        ი = i (machine)
  ე = e (pet)           ო = o (note)
  ი = i (machine)       უ = u (rule)
  გ = g (go)            ქ = k (king)
  დ = d (dog)           ლ = l (love)
  ვ = v (very)          მ = m (man)
  ზ = z (zebra)         ნ = n (no)
  თ = t (top)           პ = p' (emphatic p)
  ი = i (machine)       ჟ = zh (measure)
  კ = k' (emphatic k)   რ = r (rolled)
  ლ = l (love)          ს = s (see)
  მ = m (man)           ტ = t' (emphatic t)
  ნ = n (no)            უ = u (rule)
  ფ = p (spin)          ქ = k (king)
  ღ = gh (Arabic gh)     ყ = q (Semitic q)
  შ = sh (ship)         ჩ = ch (church)
  ც = ts (cats)         ძ = dz (adze)
  წ = ts' (emphatic)    ჭ = ch' (emphatic)
  ხ = kh (Scottish loch) ჯ = j (jump)
  ჰ = h (hat)           ჱ = ē (archaic)
  ჲ = y (yes)           ჳ = wī (archaic)
  ჴ = q' (archaic)      ჵ = hō (archaic)
